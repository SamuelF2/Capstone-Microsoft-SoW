# Analysis of SDMPlus and Its Personas in the MCEM for ISD (Chrysalis)

**SDMPlus** (Service Delivery Methodology Plus) has historically been the *Industry Solutions Delivery* (ISD) **process repository** – a one-stop knowledge base for Microsoft's consulting delivery methodology, including processes, templates, and roles & responsibilities. Over the past two years, SDMPlus has undergone significant changes to integrate with Microsoft's unified **Microsoft Customer Engagement Methodology (MCEM)** and to consolidate knowledge onto a new platform called **Chrysalis**. This analysis explains SDMPlus's evolution into **"MCEM for ISD"** on Chrysalis and details the key personas (user groups) associated with it – **knowledge consumers**, **content contributors/owners**, **platform administrators**, and **program stakeholders** – including each persona's responsibilities, workflows, and interactions with the platform.

## SDMPlus/Chrysalis Overview (Current State)

**Chrysalis** is now the single home for ISD knowledge, consolidating what used to be separate platforms (SDMPlus for process guidance, SEE for project assets, Knowledge Garden/Eureka for IP kits). The **"MCEM for ISD"** section on Chrysalis contains **all ISD process guidance** that was on SDMPlus – organized by MCEM stages (Listen & Consult, Inspire & Design, etc., through Realize Value and Manage & Optimize) – as well as related templates and tools. This consolidation, led by the **ISD Excellence & Partner team**, aimed to provide a *"single, smart platform"* with a consistent experience for all ISD employees.

The content itself remains aligned to the **MCEM methodology** (Microsoft's end-to-end sales-through-delivery process). Users can navigate by MCEM stage or use AI-powered search within Chrysalis to quickly find what they need. The knowledge artifacts include detailed process descriptions, **step-by-step activities**, **RACI charts** (Responsible/Accountable/Consulted/Informed matrices), **job aids**, and links to policies or tools. For example, a Consulting Project Manager can find **delivery management** procedures under the "Realize Value" stage, complete with guidance on tasks, owners, and templates to use. All **document templates** (e.g. project plan, risk register, handoff deck templates) that were on SDMPlus are now under the **Assets** tab in Chrysalis for easy access.

Importantly, the move to Chrysalis did *not* change the content itself – it's an **integration and rebranding**. Existing process content and templates were carried over intact (just reorganized under MCEM). The **user experience** was designed to feel familiar to SDMPlus users, and the legacy links (like **aka.ms/sdmplus**) continue to work via automatic redirect. What **did** change is the ease of discovery (one platform instead of several) and the inclusion of **all ISD roles** as target users, not just delivery roles. This expansion was already in motion with the MCEM alignment in early 2024 – at that time SDMPlus became "fully MCEM-aligned … for all roles in the sales and delivery lifecycle," whereas previously it focused mainly on delivery practitioners.

Below, we identify and describe the main personas involved with SDMPlus/"MCEM for ISD" on Chrysalis, including their roles, typical responsibilities, and how each interacts with the platform. These personas range from the frontline **consumers** of the content, to the **contributors** who maintain and improve it, to the **administrators** and **stakeholders** who support and govern the system.

## Personas in SDMPlus (MCEM for ISD) and Their Roles

### 1. **Knowledge Consumers (Field Users of MCEM/ISD Content)**

**Who they are:** The vast majority of SDMPlus/Chrysalis users are **practitioners in the field** who consume the process content. This includes **delivery roles** (e.g. Consulting Project Managers, Architects, Delivery Management Executives, Client Delivery Partners, Engineers) and **sales/pursuit roles** (e.g. Consulting Account Executives, Solution Specialists, Pursuit Leads, Sales Managers), as well as supporting roles like quality or operations managers. In fact, *"all ISD roles"* across the sales–delivery lifecycle are now served by the MCEM for ISD repository. Even ISD leadership and management personnel fall into this category when they reference the methodology for governance or training purposes (the January 2024 update explicitly listed **Pursuit ICs, Sales managers, Solution Architects, ISD Leadership,** and many others as impacted users).

**Responsibilities & Needs:** These consumers are responsible for **executing their part of the engagement process** in alignment with Microsoft's standards. They rely on SDMPlus/Chrysalis to understand **"how to" steps, best practices, and deliverables** for each phase of an engagement. For example:

*   A **Pursuit Lead** in pre-sales will consult the *Listen & Consult* and *Inspire & Design* sections to follow the sales-to-delivery handoff process or to prepare for Opportunity Review Board requirements.
*   A **Project Manager** in delivery will use the *Realize Value* section to follow project initiation steps, risk management processes, quality gates, etc., and to download standard templates (e.g. a **Project Structure document** or **Risk Management Plan**).
*   Team members look at **RACI matrices** and role descriptions in the guidance to clarify who is accountable for what in each activity (e.g. a Pursuit Lead sees that during handoff, they must schedule the meeting and ensure a proper briefing to the delivery team).

In practice, field users interact with the platform by **searching or browsing** for relevant topics. The Chrysalis platform's AI-powered search allows them to quickly find content by keywords (for instance, searching "S2D handoff" or "ACR tracking" yields the specific process pages). Users can also navigate via the MCEM stages menu or the "Full Scope Explorer" to find the guidance corresponding to a particular engagement stage. Once on the content, they typically:

*   **Read process steps and checklists:** ensuring they follow required activities in the proper sequence.
*   **Identify their role's tasks via RACI:** The content often lists roles and responsibilities for each activity (e.g. who is *Responsible* vs. *Accountable* for a given task). A user will scan these to understand their duties and coordination points with other roles.
*   **Download templates and tools:** Consumers frequently pull down Word/Excel/PowerPoint templates for deliverables (like kickoff decks, status report templates, etc.) from the Assets library or via links embedded in process pages.
*   **Use job aids or checklists:** Many pages include job aid documents or checklists (for example, an initiation checklist, ORB checklist, etc.) that practitioners use to ensure compliance.
*   **Stay updated on process changes:** By using Chrysalis (which is kept up-to-date by the content owners), field members automatically follow the latest approved process. The consolidation into Chrysalis helps in this regard – previously, users had to jump between **SDMPlus** (for methodology) and other sites like **SEE** (for project lessons or assets) and **Knowledge Garden** (for Business Apps knowledge), but now these are unified. This means less confusion and faster onboarding for new team members, who can find everything in one place. As the announcement described, *"new joiners get up-to-speed faster… Teams across regions share and reuse assets more easily"* now that all knowledge is centralized.

Overall, **Consumers** treat SDMPlus/MCEM for ISD as the **"how-to manual"** for executing projects and deals. Following this guidance is often mandated or at least strongly encouraged in ISD; for instance, delivery quality controls (like TQA or RMQA checks) may verify that teams adhered to the processes. Thus, these users have the responsibility to consult the repository throughout an engagement's lifecycle and ensure their work products align with the provided templates and standards. In summary, **field consumers use SDMPlus/Chrysalis to **execute their roles effectively and consistently**, leveraging it for process steps, role clarity, and standardized documentation.

### 2. **Process Content Owners (Methodology Authors & SMEs)**

**Who they are:** Content owners are the **subject matter experts** and **knowledge managers** who create and maintain the process documentation. They typically belong to the **ISD Excellence & Partner team or a related central team** responsible for methodology. In many cases, these are experienced **Delivery Managers, Program Leads, or architects** assigned as "feature owners" for parts of the methodology. For example, one person might own the *Initiate phase* content, another owns *Scrum methodology adaptations*, another owns *Secure Delivery requirements*, etc. Before integration into Chrysalis, these individuals maintained the SDMPlus site (often via SharePoint pages or documents). Now they continue that role within Chrysalis as the content curators for the MCEM for ISD section.

**Responsibilities & Workflows:** Content owners' primary responsibility is to **keep the methodology accurate, up-to-date, and aligned with organizational changes**. They author new guidance, update existing pages for process improvements or policy changes, and manage the overall information architecture (ensuring content is logically organized under the MCEM stages). Specific duties include:

*   **Authoring and updating content:** They write the detailed process steps, RACI charts, and guidance for each activity. If a new initiative arises (e.g. a new security requirement or a refined sales process), they will document the associated process in Chrysalis. For instance, when *Secure by Default* practices were introduced, content owners updated the ISRA (Information Security Risk Assessment) procedures and roles in the repository.
*   **Incorporating feedback and improvements:** SDMPlus has a built-in *crowdsourcing* or collaboration mechanism for process improvements. Field users can submit suggestions (via a "Suggest Edit" or an ideas panel), which content owners then review. Part of the content owner's workflow is to triage these suggestions and decide whether to incorporate them. In SDMPlus, an editorial process existed: a user would *"Select the process area... call out the changes in SDM+ and 'Suggest Edit',"* and the **Feature Owner** of that content would receive the suggestion. If the feature owner accepts it, the change goes live in SDMPlus/Chrysalis. This continues today in Chrysalis – it supports **crowdsourced contributions** while ensuring a content owner approves changes before publishing.
*   **Governance and alignment:** Content owners ensure that the processes in the repository stay aligned with **MCEM** and any **MCAPS** (Microsoft Consulting and Product Services) standards. For example, as MCEM evolves or as leadership decides new focus areas (consumption tracking, new review boards, etc.), the owners update the repository so that field practices reflect those changes. They often work closely with the ISD Excellence program leads to plan these updates (e.g., quarterly process refreshes, integration of new tools like Virtuoso or CompassOne steps into the methodology).
*   **Content organization and tagging:** On Chrysalis, they might also be responsible for categorizing content correctly, so that search and AI agents (like the Chrysalis Copilot) can find information easily.

**Interactions with the Platform:** These users have **edit access** to the Chrysalis "MCEM for ISD" section. They use SharePoint-like interfaces to edit pages or upload documents. Their interaction loop often involves: drafting changes (perhaps offline in Word or directly on the site), publishing updates, and communicating significant changes via release notes or Full Scope announcements. SDMPlus used to allow publishing *"Full Scope"* news (as we saw with the January 2024 and Sept 2025 Full Scope posts) to inform everyone of changes. Content owners often coordinate such communications when major updates occur (for instance, if a new **Delivery Phase** is added or a template is changed, they'll broadcast it).

Importantly, content owners act as **gatekeepers** of quality. They are typically the **"Feature Owners"** mentioned in the process: only after a feature owner approves a suggested edit does it get reflected to all users. This ensures consistency and authority of the content, even though the contribution model is collaborative. For example, if an Engagement Lead in the field suggests improving a checklist, the designated owner (maybe a Delivery IP team member) reviews it, possibly discusses with a wider methodology board, and then updates the official guidance. This governance model is part of the **crowdsourcing functionality** – encouraging contributions but with oversight.

Thus, the **Process Content Owner persona** interacts with SDMPlus/Chrysalis in a **content management capacity**. Their success is measured by keeping the repository accurate, comprehensive, and useful. In practice, they might have a backlog of content updates (driven by user feedback, quarterly process changes, or leadership directives) and use the platform's editing and publishing tools to execute those. They also respond to inquiries about content (sometimes field users might reach out on Yammer/Teams or through the Chrysalis support alias with questions or errata, which the content owners address). In summary, this persona **ensures the knowledge base remains the "single source of truth" for ISD methodologies**, balancing contributions from the community with careful curation.

### 3. **Field Contributors (Practitioners Suggesting Improvements)**

**Who they are:** In addition to formal content owners, **regular users in the field can act as contributors** by providing feedback, corrections, or new ideas for the process content. This persona overlaps with the *consumer* persona (since contributors are also typically consumers of the content), but it highlights those users who take an **active role in improving the repository**. They could be experienced Project Managers, Architects, or any team member who spots a gap or inefficiency in the documented process and wants to suggest an update.

**Responsibilities & Behavior:** While contributing improvements is not an obligatory part of a field role, Microsoft's culture encourages **"crowd sourcing" of knowledge and continuous improvement** of processes. These contributors take it upon themselves to share lessons learned and better practices back to the central repository. For example, after completing a project, a Consulting PM might realize that a certain risk management step could be enhanced – they might then use the **Collaboration Panel** or *Suggest Edit* feature to propose that enhancement in SDMPlus. Also, as noted in internal documentation, *"every quarter, lessons learned for each area are captured and shared with stakeholders… submit SDMPlus improvement ideas through its Collaboration Panel"*. In many teams, retrospective meetings explicitly include a step to feed ideas into SDMPlus (now Chrysalis). Thus, some field contributors fulfill a **feedback loop responsibility** as part of project closure or continuous improvement initiatives.

**How they interact with the platform:** SDMPlus (and now Chrysalis) provides mechanisms for user feedback:

*   A **"Suggest Edit"** button on process pages allows users to propose content changes. As described in the process documentation, the user selects the relevant process area, outlines the suggested edits, and submits them. The suggestion is routed to the content owner for review.
*   A **dedicated Ideas or Feedback panel** (Chrysalis has an Ideas portal, and SDMPlus had a Collaboration Panel) for more general suggestions. For instance, **Chrysalis Ideas** platform might collect suggestions that span beyond a single page, and the SDMPlus panel did similarly for process improvement ideas.
*   Some contributors may simply email or message the Chrysalis support or content owners with their input, especially if the change is complex.

Once submitted, a field contributor's idea might result in an update. If it's accepted, the content owner will publish the change and often credit the contributor or at least communicate that the process was updated thanks to field feedback. This persona's interaction is therefore occasional and *ad hoc*, triggered by specific insights or pain points they encounter. However, these contributions are vital to keep the methodology relevant. Microsoft's consulting practices are diverse, so field feedback ensures SDMPlus content covers real-world scenarios.

**Summary:** Field Contributors don't have formal "write" permissions to publish content directly, but they **interact via feedback tools to influence the content**. Their "responsibility" is essentially proactive: by contributing, they help improve the collective guidance for everyone. In doing so, they often work in conjunction with content owners (who may reach out for clarification on suggestions). This persona exemplifies the **crowd-sourced knowledge model** of SDMPlus – moving it from a static one-way repository to a living, community-enriched knowledge base. It's a stakeholder group in the sense that their voice can drive changes to processes that thousands of others will later follow.

### 4. **Platform Administrators & Support Team**

**Who they are:** With the move to Chrysalis, a dedicated **Chrysalis Support Team** exists to administer the platform and support users. This persona includes **site administrators, IT support engineers, and product managers for Chrysalis**. They are responsible for the technical functioning of the Chrysalis platform (which hosts not only MCEM for ISD but other knowledge areas). Before consolidation, SDMPlus was run on a SharePoint-based site (with likely support from a smaller platform team); now the scope is larger, and a unified support team oversees it.

**Responsibilities:** The administrators have several key responsibilities:

*   **Platform maintenance and upgrades:** Ensure the Chrysalis site is up, performant, and secure. They handle tasks like managing user access (though most ISD employees should have default access as readers), fixing any broken links or permissions (especially after the migration, making sure redirects from *aka.ms/SDMPlus* and old bookmarks correctly point to new pages), and implementing new features (for example, integrating the AI search capabilities or enabling new feedback tools).
*   **Content migration and organization:** The support team was instrumental in physically moving SDMPlus content into Chrysalis. This included copying pages, preserving their formatting, setting up the **"MCEM for ISD"** tab structure, and verifying that all templates went into the Assets repository. Administrators likely worked with content owners during this migration (for quality checks) and continue to assist if any content needs re-tagging or if new sections are added.
*   **User support (helpdesk):** They serve as the helpdesk for any issues or questions related to Chrysalis. The **Chrysalis support team** can be reached via a support email (e.g. *<chrysalissupport@microsoft.com>*). Users might contact them if they encounter access issues, page errors, or have usage questions on the platform. For example, if a consultant can't find a template that used to be on SDMPlus, they might reach out to support – the support team would guide them (perhaps "that template is now under Assets > Delivery Templates") or fix any oversight in migration.
*   **Moderation and governance:** While content governance is mainly the content owners' domain, the platform admins ensure that the **process documentation support** is handled. For instance, an internal change document notes that *"support for MCEM process documentation will be provided by the Chrysalis Support team"* – this implies that any user queries or issues with the process content (navigation, search, etc.) are fielded by this team as first line, even if the resolution may involve the content owner. They likely triage feedback: e.g., distinguishing "how do I do X in Chrysalis?" (a support question) from "the content on this page is outdated" (which they would pass to the content owner/SME).
*   **Training and onboarding:** Administrators might produce quick guides or *"landing site"* info for users to learn the new system. Indeed, users were pointed to a landing page (aka.ms/KnowledgeInChrysalis) for a walking deck, videos, and FAQs about the move. The support/admin team likely prepared these resources and held sessions (like the *Full Scope Office Hours*) to answer user questions.

**Interactions:** Admins interact with the platform in a **backend capacity** – managing site settings, running analytics (perhaps to monitor usage metrics and search effectiveness), and receiving support tickets. They engage directly with users through support channels. For example, if a user emails the support alias, an admin will respond and help. They also interact with content owners: if a content owner needs a new page created or encounters a technical glitch, they collaborate to fix it. Additionally, the admin team supports features like the **Chrysalis AI assistant (Copilot)** which is meant to help navigate knowledge; ensuring that is working for the MCEM for ISD content may fall under their duties.

In summary, this persona **ensures the infrastructure and user support for SDMPlus/MCEM content is in place**. Since Chrysalis is an internal product, they sit at the intersection of IT and the business content. They uphold the *"one platform"* vision by keeping Chrysalis reliable and user-friendly. As noted, *"All support for MCEM process documentation is now handled by the Chrysalis Support team"*, highlighting that this team is now critical for any issues that previously might have been addressed in scattered ways when multiple portals existed. Their ultimate goal is to guarantee that end users and content owners can do their jobs on Chrysalis smoothly, thereby facilitating knowledge flow within ISD.

### 5. **ISD Excellence & Leadership (Program Stakeholders)**

**Who they are:** This persona represents the **stakeholder groups who oversee the methodology and its adoption** at an organizational level – primarily the **Industry Solutions Delivery Excellence team**, along with ISD **Leadership** (directors and managers in the delivery organization). The ISD Excellence & Partner team spearheaded the consolidation initiative (as evidenced by their role in "bringing it all together in Chrysalis") and continues to guide the strategy for knowledge management. ISD Leadership (like regional delivery leads, practice leads, etc.) are stakeholders because the methodology directly impacts how their teams execute projects and engage with customers.

**Responsibilities and Interests:**

*   **Strategic direction:** These stakeholders decide on *"what is the methodology and how should it evolve?"* They align SDMPlus content with **business priorities**. For instance, if acceleration of Azure consumption is a priority, leadership ensures the repository emphasizes consumption tracking processes. The Excellence team might maintain a **process roadmap**, deciding what new content to add (e.g., if a new delivery risk process is needed) or what obsolete content to prune.
*   **Governance and compliance:** Leadership expects that projects follow the MCEM for ISD processes. They might set KPIs for methodology adoption (for example, requiring that 100% of projects have a delivery methodology selected in Virtuoso and followed via SDMPlus guidance). The repository serves as the official policy, so ensuring people use it is part of their concern. They will receive reports or feedback on how often content is accessed, or which areas generate many questions, indicating where clarity or training is needed.
*   **Championing knowledge culture:** The Excellence team often runs **campaigns, training, and communications** to encourage using Chrysalis. The Full Scope announcements (monthly communications) that highlighted SDMPlus changes are an example: leadership used those to broadcast the new repository interface and later the migration to Chrysalis. They also organize things like **Office Hours** (to take questions about the changes) and produce **"snackable videos"**, FAQs, and quick start guides (indeed a *"FY25 MCEM for ISD Quick Start Guide"* was published to introduce the methodology).
*   **Ensuring content quality and completeness:** While content owners do the hands-on writing, leadership stakeholders make sure all process areas have an owner and that the content remains comprehensive. They may form a **governance board** or regular review cycle for the content. For example, each quarter, the Excellence team might review any new ideas submitted and approve which to implement, in line with business strategy. They ensure the **RACI roles listed in the processes reflect the actual org structure and roles** in ISD – notice that when SDMPlus expanded to sales, it explicitly pulled in sales roles into the impacted list, which likely required coordination among delivery and sales leadership.
*   **Interfacing with other programs:** These stakeholders connect the repository with other tools and programs. MCEM for ISD is one part of a larger ecosystem; leadership coordinates with owners of systems like CompassOne, MSX, or Virtuoso to make sure processes and tools are in sync. (For instance, if Virtuoso introduces a new feature for project status, the process documentation should mention it – this alignment is often driven by program managers in the Excellence team.)

**Interactions:** The ISD Excellence team and leadership interact with the platform mostly through **analytics and content reviews**, rather than daily usage of content for project work. They will look at how the content is being used: metrics like page views, search terms, or areas with frequent support questions. They may solicit feedback from the field via surveys or discussions and use Chrysalis as a channel to broadcast updates. In Chrysalis, they have permissions to post news articles (Full Scope updates) and to manage some high-level content pages (like overview pages, landing pages). They also interact with the **support team** – for example, to get reports on usage or to coordinate the rollout of new sections.

During the migration to Chrysalis, these stakeholders ensured the change was well-communicated and phased appropriately (the snippet shows Effective date Aug 27, 2025, indicating leadership orchestrated the timing and announcement of the migration). They also likely handle budgeting and resources for the platform (funding the support team and development of Chrysalis features).

In summary, **ISD Excellence & Leadership** personas see SDMPlus/Chrysalis as a crucial tool to drive **consistent, high-quality execution across the global ISD organization**. They are accountable for the success of this knowledge system. Their interactions are at the management level: setting direction for content, ensuring people know about and use the platform, and measuring its impact. Thanks to their efforts, **finding the right knowledge is now easier and unified** for practitioners, which in turn supports ISD's business goals (faster ramp-up, improved delivery, and cross-team reuse of IP).

## Conclusion

**Internal Usage Patterns & Integration:** In practice, these personas collaborate to make SDMPlus (now Chrysalis MCEM for ISD) a living, useful repository. A typical scenario might involve a **Project Manager (consumer)** accessing a checklist, then realizing an improvement and becoming a **contributor** by suggesting it, which a **content owner** reviews and accepts, with the help of an **admin** to deploy the change – all under the watch of **Excellence leaders** who ensure such feedback loops exist. With SDMPlus's content now fully on Chrysalis, users have noticed the convenience of not "jumping between platforms" and benefitted from quicker search (thanks to AI). Meanwhile, stakeholder teams have a unified view to manage knowledge. Thus, the integration into Chrysalis has reinforced the **roles of each persona**: consumers have everything in one place, contributors can influence more visibly, content owners manage one consolidated library, admins support a single platform, and leaders drive one knowledge strategy.

Overall, **SDMPlus (MCEM for ISD)** has evolved from a standalone process repository into an **integral part of Chrysalis**, serving as the backbone of methodology guidance for Industry Solutions Delivery. The personas from everyday consultants to program leaders each play a part in this ecosystem:

*   **Consumers** use the knowledge to deliver projects successfully and consistently.
*   **Contributors and Owners** jointly ensure the knowledge remains current and relevant (utilizing built-in crowdsourcing features).
*   **Administrators** keep the engine running and help users navigate the new unified platform.
*   **Leadership stakeholders** ensure this knowledge platform delivers value and aligns with the organization's processes and culture.
