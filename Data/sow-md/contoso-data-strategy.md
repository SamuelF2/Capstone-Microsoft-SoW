# Introduction

Currently, the Contoso Inc (Contoso Inc) is collecting, creating and managing large and ever-growing amounts of data. This data comes from many sources including, but not limited to: traffic sensors and controllers, highway winter maintenance vehicles, 407 transponders, road weather cameras, images, social media, satellites, traditional data sources, and in the near future, connected and automated vehicles. To fully leverage and integrate these data sources and meet the strategic directions and needs of tomorrow, a ministry-wide data and information strategy is needed.

Contoso Inc’s Data and Information Strategy (Contoso Inc Data Strategy), an output of the first phase of MCS work established the foundation required to create an organization that is data-driven, innovative, client focused and future ready. Contoso Inc Data Strategy will empower Contoso Inc to capitalize on existing data, improve information sharing, and develop better analytics to enhance government decision making. Contoso Inc wants to:

- Unlock the Power of Data – Unlocking the full power of the data by Contoso Inc to make evidence-based decisions, support digital transformations, accelerate innovation, improve client experience, and deliver greater value to Ontarians through new and improved products and services.
- The Need to Lead - To be a leader in transportation is to be digital and data-driven. To attract talent and industry to Ontario, Contoso Inc must embrace new technologies, such as data and analytics. Data and analytics provide the capability to identify, understand and address transportation challenges and opportunities, empowering evidence-based decisions and optimized investments.
- Drive Operational Efficiency - As Contoso Inc becomes more adept at turning data into insights, many opportunities will emerge to use insights and analytics to redesign business processes, eliminate delays and enable the effective and efficient use of resources.
- Be Future Ready - There are many new elements altering the transportation landscape and creating even more data, including connected and automated vehicles, population growth, the changing travel habits of the younger generation, climate change and the sharing economy. There is a real need to develop capacity to deal with these changes and data volumes.
In the first phase the Data Strategy development, Contoso Inc partnered with Microsoft to develop the Contoso Inc Data Strategy strategy framework which is organized into four high level areas referred to as Compass Directions. Each Compass Direction is further divided into Compass Headings as depicted below.

Alongside this Contoso Inc initiative, 23 capabilities across the four Compass Headings were identified and prioritized to define a Minimal Viable Product (MVP) 1.0, the first release of Contoso Inc Data Strategy. Contoso Inc Data Strategy MVP 1.0 focuses primarily on implementing Priority 1 (P1) and some P2 capabilities. The full set of capabilities will be iteratively activated over specific strategic horizons following Contoso Inc’s executive approval, but allow for adjustments, integration with other parallel capability activations, and to provide responsiveness to evolving business goals.

Contoso Inc Data Strategy strategic horizons are:

| --- | --- |
| Short-term actions: | 91 days to 6 months |
| Medium-term actions: | 7 months to 12 months |
| Long-term actions: | 13 months to 18+ months |

- Sprints that produce a working solution.

### Delivery sprints

Each delivery sprint will last two weeks. The final duration for sprints will be determined in collaboration with Contoso Inc during Engagement Baseline Planning.

Before sprint planning starts, Contoso Inc product owner will collaborate with Microsoft to create a proposed sprint scope. This sprint scope will consist of a set of user stories that Microsoft and the product owner estimate may be completed during the sprint.

The first day of every sprint will be set aside for Sprint Planning for that sprint. (In some exceptional cases, sprint planning may extend past the first day.) The Microsoft delivery team and Contoso Inc product owner will attend. The Microsoft team will lead the meeting and the following activities will take place:

- Each user story will be reviewed by the Microsoft delivery team. The Microsoft delivery team will determine if there is sufficient information to begin delivery. The Microsoft team might seek clarification from Contoso Inc Product Owner. If there is insufficient information to develop a story and the Product Owner cannot provide clarification during the meeting, the story might be deferred to a later sprint or the solution backlog by the Microsoft delivery team.
- The Microsoft delivery team will determine which user stories can be accomplished during the sprint. If the proposed scope is too large, the team will collaborate with Contoso Inc Product Owner to defer stories to a later sprint or the solution backlog. If the proposed scope is too small, the team will collaborate with Contoso Inc Product Owner to add user stories. The user stories selected for the sprint are solely determined by the Microsoft delivery team.
- The Microsoft delivery team will determine the technical feasibility of a user story. If the technical feasibility of a user story requires further investigation, a corresponding backlog item might be added to investigate potential solutions. If the Microsoft delivery team determines that a user story is not feasible, the story can be removed from the backlog by the Microsoft delivery team.
- The Microsoft delivery team will work to decide how the work will be accomplished. This might include design discussions, updates to the architecture, and a breakdown of user stories into tasks.
During the sprint, the delivery team will build out the solution with planned user stories and architecture, which will be updated, if it is required. Daily standup meetings will be performed by the delivery team to keep everyone informed and to report any impediments.

During the sprint, if the Microsoft delivery team determines that a backlog item cannot be completed within the sprint duration, it will be deferred to a later sprint after consultation with the team and Contoso Inc product owner. If the delivery team has extra capacity in a sprint, the delivery team will collaborate with the product owner to select backlog items to be added to the sprint scope. Microsoft is the sole decision maker on scope changes once the sprint has started.

The last day of the sprint is usually dedicated to demonstrating the functionality that has been achieved in the sprint and to carrying out a retrospective of the sprint. This is conducted in two parts.

- Sprint review: a sprint review meeting is held at the end of the sprint to inspect the increment and adapt the product backlog if needed. The product owner and Customer stakeholders will attend to foster collaboration and provide appropriate feedback.
- Sprint retrospective: the sprint retrospective is an opportunity for the scrum team to inspect itself and determine if there are any improvements that need to be enacted during the next sprint.
